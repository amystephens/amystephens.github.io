Soda Springs Nordic Ski Association

by Summer Wheatley

Soda Springs boasts some of the best cross country skiing in the area, possibly the whole world. Just five minutes from downtown Soda Springs, Kelly Park offers about four miles of cross country ski trails. The park has beautiful views of the valley where Soda Spring sits. Be on the lookout for spring-fed creeks, lakes, several varieties of trees and even our local herd of elk.

The trails are set up in an interwoven series of loops that allow all levels of skiers to either train or just enjoy the area. Ski trails are groomed with quality equipment leaving the snow in perfect condition.

The trails are situated at about 5,800 feet in elevation and typically have snow on the ground from December through March. Be aware that the trails are unmarked and not patrolled. Dogs are allowed, but please pick up their poopies. In case you are not familiar with cross country skiing etiquette, it is frowned upon to camp, dig snow caves, walk or even snowshoe on classic ski tracks.

Skier donations fund the care of these beautiful trails. You can support the Kelly Park Ski Trail System by putting money in the dropbox near the pavillion. Cash or checks are accepted. You can also drop your credit card in if that is more convenient. Venmo is not accepted at this time because we are about ten years behind the rest of the world. It is recommended that skiers donate $3 per day on the trails or $25 dollars per season. Really what it comes down to is don't be a cheapskate and give us some money because nothing in life is free.
